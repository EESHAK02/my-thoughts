Almost There, Always

March 2026

And yet again, the finish line moved.

It happens quietly enough that you almost miss it. You spend weeks ramping up on a new something - a model, a framework, a way of thinking about a problem, and just as the ground starts to feel solid, three new releases land in a single week and it feels like the ground beneath you just slipped away. Not gone. Just relocated, again, slightly further than where you were standing.

The pace

It's as if the field has picked a rhythm of a kind. One morning, a new model comes out, and before anyone has finished writing the long-form analysis of what it means, an agent framework built on top of it ships too. Then a benchmark gets beaten. But guess what? Probably the benchmark didn't measure the right thing to begin with. That makes way for another benchmark. None of this is dramatic on its own. It's the cumulative effect that leaves you lost - the feeling that understanding something fully is perhaps a far-fetched goal, because by the time you would, you're already behind - because something more exciting is always ready in the pipeline.

It's tempting to call this exhausting, and some days it is. But it's also just the way the world is moving right now. The question remains - what's in it for you? Perhaps it's like weather - you can't really address it, but you dress for it. And that's exactly what we are learning - how to adapt to and evolve with the ever-changing landscape of this field.

Some context for context retrieval

Take retrieval-augmented generation for instance, a clean example of how even already solved is always a step behind of what can be solved! What started as a simple approach - chunk a document, embed it, store it, and when a question comes in, find the chunks that are closest matches and hand them to a model. Blind similarity, essentially. Structure? Relationships? Nothing at that time, really. But somehow it worked, well enough to give meaningful answers.

And then, almost immediately, the flaws began to surface. Pure similarilty often meant missing things that mattered - the one fact phrased differently, that crucial connection in an unconnected chunk,... Hybrid retrieval arrived, the idea of keyword search was baked in, making way for advanced ideas like graph-based approaches, because flat chunks couldn't capture the relationships between ideas scattered across documents. And was that it? No, agentic retrieval arrived, where we move beyond just fetch and hand over it can reason about whether what it found is actually sufficient, and if not, figure out what's required.

The same three letters, RAG, but one small change to send ripples through the field with every new development.

And somewhere in the middle of all those advancements, a quieter question began revolving - does retrieval even hold the same place anymore? With huge context windows now accessible, the very reason why RAG existed stopped being a problem for certain use cases. Did the need disappear? Perhaps not - but did it find new shapes to live in? 101% yes - it has turned more towards retrieval for memory, for reasoning.

The middle, permanently

How do I even put this into a before-after story? There are still people using simple RAG, there are use-cases where it's all fitting in that one window - so no RAG but all the information is there, and then there's agentic RAG taking over the others. That makes me question: are we catching up to a destinaton? or just running to find the right direction?

That's where I feel we enter a spiral - not behind, not ahead, but just revolving around finish lines that keep moving, but also never really disappear - we reach a point only to relise this can go deeper. And at every turn is a tangent - a new approach, a new question that we probably never thought of asking before.

Maybe that's the privilege of working in something this alive right now - the chance to be a part of the process of not only finding answers, but also newer and better questions. Unfolding something that refuses to be fully known in all its colors, all at once - feels like a mystery, not to be solved but to be lived through. The subltle clues into what's possible, the excitement of experimentation, the joy of discovery - it's all where we are - right in the middle of things.

Almost there, always - arriving somewhere, only to find out that the somewhere is a part of somewhere else.
